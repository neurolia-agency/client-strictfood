# Template Sublimation — Burger StrictFood

## Usage

```
--input-image photos-brutes/produits-seuls/burgers/[fichier].jpeg
```

## Prompt

Transform this raw kitchen photo into a premium dark food photograph with tack-sharp, pin-sharp focus across the entire burger, from the closest sesame seed to the furthest leaf of mâche, achieved through a small aperture equivalent to f/11–f/16 delivering maximum depth of field with every plane in crisp focus. Replace the existing background with a deep, dark charcoal surface that fades seamlessly into shadow, consistent with StrictFood's signature dark aesthetic. Bathe the entire scene in broad, overhead, warm-toned soft light at low intensity, perfectly symmetrical left to right, wrapping evenly around every layer of the burger so that highlights and shadows stay gentle and uniform from bottom bun to top bun. Unify the color palette into a cohesive warm temperature where every ingredient feels like it belongs in the same beautifully lit environment.

Preserve every ingredient exactly as it appears in the input — same texture, same color, same quantity, same spatial placement — while reading the stack from bottom to top to lock each layer in place. The base is a round charcoal bun dusted with golden sesame seeds, its surface matte-dark with a subtle pillowy compression where the steak rests. Above it sits a thick beef patty showing a rugged, irregular Maillard crust in deep amber-brown tones, seared by pulsed heat with a purely seared surface free of linear marks. Scattered over the patty, irregular crumbles and fine powder of parmesan catch the warm light in pale ivory-gold, some pieces chunky, others dusty. Thin concentric rings of red onion in translucent ruby-purple drape gently across the parmesan. A bouquet of small, round mâche leaves in bright tender green sits above the onions, some leaves peeking past the edge of the bun. A subtle filet of yellow-orange pepper sauce threads between the layers, visible in glimpses rather than pooling. The top charcoal bun mirrors the base, dark and round, crowned with the same golden sesame seeds.

Elevate the appetizing quality of every surface by sharpening micro-details — the grain of the Maillard crust, the veining on each mâche leaf, the translucent layers of onion rings, individual sesame seeds catching light — while keeping proportions compact and realistic, true to the fast-food scale visible in the input. Frame the burger in a square 1:1 crop, centered horizontally, occupying roughly sixty to sixty-five percent of the frame height, with breathing room above and below. Allow organic imperfections to remain: a slightly asymmetric lean, a leaf or two extending beyond the bun, a sesame seed on the surface — these details signal authenticity and make the image feel real rather than rendered.
