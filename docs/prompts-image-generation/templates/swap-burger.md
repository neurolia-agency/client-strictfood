GUARDRAIL — Proportions:
The patty must have realistic proportions relative to the bun. The patty diameter should be roughly equal to or just slightly wider than the bun — never massively wider. The patty thickness should be moderate and believable (about 1.5-2cm thick for a single patty), NOT an oversized restaurant-style monster patty. The overall burger height should feel like something you can actually bite into. Keep realistic fast-food scale, not exaggerated gourmet proportions. The burger should appear compact and modestly sized in the frame — leave generous breathing space around it, do NOT let the burger fill the entire image.

GUARDRAIL — Sauce placement:
The sauce must be present in TWO locations: (1) a moderate drizzle visible between the bottom bun and the patty — enough to be clearly seen but not dripping or pooling excessively, and (2) between the toppings and the top bun. Both layers should look natural, like the sauce was applied by hand during assembly.

GUARDRAIL — Natural imperfections:
The burger MUST look like a real photograph of a real burger, not a perfect CGI render. Include the kind of imperfections found in real food photography: slightly uneven ingredient placement, a leaf or two not perfectly aligned, very subtle natural asymmetry in the bun, minor sauce irregularity, slightly imperfect meat edges. The bun must have real bread texture — tiny natural bumps, uneven golden patches, NOT a smooth airbrushed dome. The leafy greens must spill out organically and loosely from the burger — NOT neatly layered or arranged in a clean row. Some leaves should overlap, some should stick out at odd angles, like a real assembled burger. Every element should have the organic roughness of real food handled by real hands. The steak surface must have a rough, rugged Maillard crust with visible charred texture and irregularities — NOT a smooth or clean patty surface.

GUARDRAIL — Frame:
Square format 1:1, centered composition. The burger should occupy roughly 60-65% of the frame height. Maintain the same framing and camera angle as the style anchor.

Avoid: deformed ingredients, duplicated leaves, floating elements, melted geometry, extra patties, text, logo, watermark, branding, any ingredient not specified in the swap instructions.
